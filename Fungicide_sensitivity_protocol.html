</p> <h2 id="fungicide-sensitivity-determination-for-fusarium-species-using-poison-plate-method">Fungicide Sensitivity Determination For <em>Fusarium</em> species using poison plate method</h2> <p>Prepared by: Jie Wang</p> <p>Version: 0.3 (July 7 2015)</p> <h3 id="background">Background</h3> <ul> <li><em>Fusarium</em> species causing sudden death syndrome or bean root rot symptoms on soybean and drybeans are of the most yield limiting plant pathogens worldwide</li> <li>Fungicide seed treatment is an effective mean of disease management strategy for SDS, especially with the fungicide fluopyram (Brandname: <a href="https://www.bayercropscience.us/products/seedgrowth/ilevo">ILeVo</a>)</li> <li>However, <em>in vitro</em> efficacy of fluopyram on <em>Fusarium</em> species growth inhibition is still not clear</li> <li>Fluopyram is a Succinate DeHydrogenase Inhibitor (SDHI) fungicide widely used in agriculture and horticulture</li> </ul> <h3 id="supplies-and-preparation">Supplies and Preparation</h3> <ol style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li>Fungicide stocks preparation (Hands-on time: 1 hr)</li> </ol> <ul> <li>Determine active ingredient according to fungicide label</li> <li><a href="http://www.agrian.com/pdfs/Luna_Privilege_Label2.pdf"><strong>Luna Privilege</strong> Label</a> Active ingredient: Fluopyram (41.5%)</li> <li>Stock solution concentrations: <strong>0, 0.5, 1, 3, 5, 7, 10, and 50 mg/mL</strong></li> <li>Detailed dilution protocol: See appendix below</li> </ul> <ol start="2" style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li>Media Preparation (Hands-on time: 2 hr, wait time: 2 hr)</li> </ol> <ul> <li>1/2 strength Potato Dextrose Agar (PDA)</li> <li>Recipe ingredients include: 12 g of Potato dextrose broth, 15 g of agar, and 1 L dH2O</li> <li><strong>[CAUTION!]</strong> Put a magnetic stir bar in the media flask to homogenize media, and be sure to leave the magnetic bar in the flask while autoclave. Turn on water bath and adjust temperature to 55ºC</li> <li>Autoclave media at 121ºC for 45 min (Wait time: 1.5~2 hr)</li> <li>When autoclaved media cool down to 55ºC, add 1 mL of fungicide stock solution into corresponding media flask and homogenize for 30 s at low speed with the magnetic bar already been put in the flask</li> </ul> <ol start="3" style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li><em>Fusarium</em> isolate transferring (Hands-on time: 1 hr, wait time: 10 d)</li> </ol> <ul> <li>Transfer 13 <em>Fusarium</em> spp. isolates (12 new isolates + 1 reference isolate Mont-1) from slants to 1/2 strength PDA media</li> <li>The most active growing point of Fungi is on the tip of a hyphae, where is the margin of the colony</li> <li>Colony cutting diagram (Figure 1)</li> </ul> <div class="figure"> <img src="/lab_protocols/img/Colony_Cubes.png" alt="Colony cutting" /> <p class="caption">Colony cutting</p> </div> <p>Cut the colony from the margin of the colony and transfer the small individual cubes to the new 1/2 strength PDA plates. The size of transferred plugs is approximately 4 mm^2 wide</p> <ul> <li>Place the cutting agar upside down on a new 1/2 PDA media</li> <li>incubate the <em>Fusarium</em> culture at 25ºC for 10 d in the dark</li> </ul> <ol start="4" style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li>Scanning protocol (Hands-on time: 40 min)</li> </ol> <ul> <li>Place six plates on the scanner, and place a <a href="http://web.ncf.ca/jim/scale/">scale bar</a> (http://web.ncf.ca/jim/scale/) on the lower corner of the scanner</li> <li>Scan file nomination systems: {Isolate Name}<em>{Concentration1}</em>{Concentration2}ppm.jpg. Use underscore (&quot; _ &quot;) only.</li> <li>For example: ARLE_C1_5_1ppm.jpg, ARLE_C1_50_10ppm.jpg, ARLE_C1_200_100ppm.jpg, and ARLE_C1_ck.jpg.</li> <li>Background color for scanning plates is dark blue (Hex-color code: #1E2E4D)</li> </ul> <h3 id="procedures">Procedures</h3> <p>Note: Detailed procedures refer to the supplies and preparation step</p> <ol style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li><p>[<strong>Day1</strong>] Make 7 L of 1/2 stength PDA media amended with 1 mL corresponding fungicide stock solutions, and pour media into 9-cM petri dishes. Normally, 1 L of 1/2 PDA media should fit with 40 petri dishes (2 bags). Let the petri dish plates air dry in the laminar flow hood overnight to remove the condensation water</p></li> <li><p>[<strong>Day2</strong>] Cut <em>Fusarium</em> colony from the margin of the colony (Figure 1), and transfer upside down on a new 1/2 strength PDA media with 3 replicates. Wrap the plate with parafilm and incubate the culture at 25ºC in a incubator. While incubating in the incubator, plates are placed upside down to avoid condensation water formed on the lids</p></li> <li><p>[<strong>Day4</strong>] Scan plates from the bottom of the plates to measure the fungal colony to measure fungal colony growth area</p></li> <li><p>[<strong>Day11</strong>] Scan plates to obtain the final area of <em>Fusarium</em> colony 10 days after transferring. Make sure data are all properly collected. Autoclave petri dishes and dispose biohazardous materials.</p></li> </ol> <h3 id="data-analysis">Data Analysis</h3> <ol style="list-style-type: decimal"> <li>All images were analyzed in APS Assess v2.0 using the manual panel and setting the HSV color space to select the colony using hue value between 0 and 195 with the dark blue background (hex color code:#1E2E4D).</li> <li>Relative mycelial growth rate was calculated by subtracting the 3 DAI colony area from the 10 DAI colony area and divided by seven days to get the colony area increase per day.</li> <li>The effective concentration to reduce growth by 50% (EC50[ subscript]) for each isolate was calculated by fit the mycelia relative growth rate against the fungicide concentrations using a 4-parameter log-logistic model with <a href="http://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/drc/index.html"><strong>drc</strong> v2.5</a></li> </ol> <h3 id="appendix">Appendix</h3> <ul> <li>Fungicide stock preparation <em>Table</em> Fungicide stock concentration preparation using Luna Privilege as an example</li> </ul> <table border="1"> <tbody> <tr> <td> Target Concentration </td> <td> Unit </td> <td> Original (mL) </td> <td> </td> <td> H2O (mL) </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 0.5 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> </td> <td> 2 mL of 10 mg/mL </td> <td> 38 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 1 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> </td> <td> 4 mL of 10 mg/mL </td> <td> 36 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 3 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> 0.24 </td> <td> </td> <td> 39.76 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 5 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> 0.4 </td> <td> </td> <td> 39.6 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 7 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> 0.56 </td> <td> </td> <td> 39.44 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 10 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> 0.8 </td> <td> </td> <td> 39.2 </td> </tr> <tr> <td> 50 </td> <td> mg/mL </td> <td> 4.01 </td> <td> </td> <td> 35.99 </td> </tr> </tbody> </table> <p>First two columns indicate the target concentrations used in the fungicide stock;
